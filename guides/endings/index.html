Endings

Endings are different ways the story of Escape the Backrooms comes to an end.

How it Works

The main idea on how Endings work is that there's one straightforward path of Levels with this path having the most amount of Levels. Reaching the end of it will give you the alternate ending "Wrong Door", however there are other separate and much shorter Level paths to get alternate endings that can only be unlocked after obtaining the Wrong Door Ending.

The Ending

The ending follows the player falling from the sky after escaping Level 55.1 and returning back to reality. Upon hitting the road, the player stops moving and presumably died due to the fall, and a Car comes up and runs the camera over.

Trivia

  • This ending is almost identical to the one shown in Kane Pixel's first Backrooms Found Footage video. You can watch it here.

The Pink House

The path to get this ending is:

  • Level 6 - Lights Out
  • Level 974
  • Ending
  • The Bunker

The Ending

After coming across the gas at the end of Level 974, the player wakes up in a much cleaner Level 974, and then the door opens when Mr. Kitty comes in and moves around until he does a hand motion and leaves the room.

The Suburbs

The path to get this ending is:

  • Level 1 - Habitable Zone
  • Level 9223372036854775807
  • Ending
  • The Bunker

The Ending

The player wakes up in a bed at home seemingly back in reality. They get out of bed, but as soon as they look out the window, it turns out they are in Level 94.

Trivia

  • This is a remastered version of the placeholder ending from the game's Release, and it still showed Level 94 because it was being worked on at the time of the placeholder ending's addition.

Empty City

The path to get this ending is:

  • The Bunker
  • Overgrowth
  • The Grassrooms
  • Ending
  • The Bunker

The Ending

Upon reaching the Elevator at the end of The Grassrooms, it starts to travel down floors and arrives to the 11th one. The doors open up to a city, which appears to be reality, however strangely enough there's no one in the city.

Trivia

  • This ending has the longest path of levels to get to the ending excluding the Wrong Door Ending.
  • The empty city seen in the ending seems to be of the Backrooms level Level 11, which is an endless city. This becomes even more clear with the fact that the Elevator arrives on the 11th floor and there is a file in the game files connected to this ending titled "BP_Level11_Exit". This might become a full level in the future, or it might just be a way of showing how big the Backrooms really are, which could also mean that the other floors the Elevator went through are other Backrooms levels.

Final Escape

This is the main ending of the game. To get this ending, you must have obtained the 4 alternate endings first. After this is done, head to the Level 3999 ending machine room and a new middle machine will be interactable. Interact with it, and you'll get the ending. If you reload your save after getting this ending, you will be in Level 3999 outside of the ending machine room.

The Ending

After looking at the Level 3999 arcade machine for a few seconds, the player turns around to see a human informing them that they aren't allowed to keep people in the arcade past 10. The arcade appears as a normal arcade now, and the human says that he'll unlock the doors and that it's been a really long day. After the screen goes dark, the human says, "Hopefully yours was better". After this, the credits roll. You'll then be sent to the lobby afterwards.

Placeholder Endings

At the end of each major update was a placeholder ending to show what was next for the game.

Release

This ending occurred right after Level 9223372036854775807. Upon jumping down the center, a cutscene would play where a man would wake up in a bed and get up and walk to the window only to spot Level 94 outside. Then, the credits would roll. This ending was remade into The Suburbs Ending.

Part 2

This ending was in Level 7. Once getting downstairs, a Boat would be right outside of the front door. Upon touching it, a cutscene would play where the player is rowing away in the boat. Then, the credits would roll.

Part 3

This ending was in Level 3999. The hallway part of the abandoned arcade hall was shrouded in darkness and upon entering it, a cutscene would play where the lights turn on showing a presumably endless hallway. Then, the credits would roll.
