Field Tactics

Note: This guide is currently outdated as it doesn't document any Version 1.0 levels or additions!

Prologue

Escape The Backrooms is an indie game made by Fancy Games. It first released on August 11th, 2022. Part 2 released on January 12th, 2023, Part 3 released on June 15th, 2023, Part 4 released on January 25th, 2024 and Part 5 released on October 23rd, 2025. In this guide, you will understand general information on every level, how to beat every level, how to handle threats, as well as information on each item. There are also a few movement techs to learn that will assist your game. If you are interested in speedrunning the game, you can find a guide for that here. This guide will only be covering information relevant to the latest version of the game, so older versions may have more or less things to be aware of.

A big part of the guide is made by Roystbit with help from Ghost, The, and CROSSANT.

Useful Knowledge And Tactics

Stamina: The duration of time you can run before you are exhausted. By default (no stamina boosts), you can run for 4 seconds straight, after which you will not be able to run for 4 seconds. Stamina is also expended as long as you are moving and holding the run key, so if you hold down the run key while you are sneaking (crouching and walking at the same time), you will become exhausted even though you did not actually move at a running speed.

There are items such as the Energy Bar and Juice that improve your stamina and running speed. The Energy Bar gives you 125 stamina points allowing you to run for 12.5 seconds straight, and the Juice allows you to sprint, running 30% faster, for 10 seconds. Moth Jelly has the benefits of both the Energy Bar and Juice.

It should also be noted that when you are in a chase sequence, you have infinite stamina until the chase is over. You should be careful expending all of your stamina when there are entities nearby (and you are not in a chase sequence yet), since if you exhaust yourself, you will need to wait to recover your stamina, even if you end up being chased while you are tired.

Sanity: A meter found in your inventory that depletes when you are in dangerous levels (but the meter does not deplete on Easy mode). Some levels deplete sanity faster than other levels, making management of your time and items essential to beating the level.

You can restore your sanity by consuming food items like Almond Water, Juice, Energy Bars, and Moth Jelly. Almond Water is the only food item that restores 50% sanity; every other food item restores 25% sanity.

If your sanity is at 15%, Smilers will appear as hallucinations around you as a warning. If your sanity runs out, then you will die even if you finish consuming something to gain sanity.

Bunny-hopping: A movement-tech that allows you to travel, such that you lose minimal stamina. You press the run key (Shift by default), jump, then you release the run key as soon as you are in the air. Once you land back on the ground, you repeat the process cyclically. Level 2 (Pipe Dreams) is a good level to practice bunny-hopping (abbreviated to "b-hopping"). With enough practice, you will be able to reach the end of the level without expending all of your stamina.

A guide for b-hopping in Escape The Backrooms can be found here.

Crouch-jumping: A movement tech that allows you to crouch after jumping, while you're in the air. You jump, then press the crouch key (Ctrl by default). You can use crouch-jumping to reach slightly higher spaces that the player is not able to land on by jumping normally.

Crouch-jumping can also be combined with b-hopping to allow you to reach more previously inaccessible areas.

View Distance at Low: By turning your View Distance all the way to the Low setting, you will have the ability to see through some walls and floors. A good example is Level 10, where the wheat-field will not render wheat until you get near it, allowing you to see some of the field, and will allow you to see Facelings that try to nab you in the beginning of the level.

Shadows at Low or Medium: By turning your Shadows all the way to the Low setting, some areas will be much easier to see due to lack of ambient lighting and shadows caused by high visual settings, and the game's performance can improve (if lag was an issue). This does also present some visual bugs, like in The Manilla Room where the shadow puzzle does not render any shadows projected by the sculpture.

Recommended Shadows settings for different levels:

Level 37 (The Poolrooms) - Medium

Reason: Visibility - limited visibility due to thick ambient "fog"

Level 94 - Medium

Reason: Visibility - limited visibility due to thick ambient fog

Level 9 (Darkened Suburbs) - Medium

Reason: Visibility - limited visibility due to thick ambient fog on Low Settings

Level 10 - Low

Reason: Visibility - limited visibility due to thick ambient fog on Medium settings or higher

Hitboxes: Although many areas are safe behind closed doors, entities can still manage to kill you if you and the entity are both against a wall or door that is thin enough. This is because the wall only prevents pathing, not attacks (especially the Aranea Membri and the Neighborhood Watch). Therefore, after reprieve from a chase you should stay away from doors and walls that the entity shares with you.

Keybinds: It is recommended to re-bind your controls if you find that the default controls are not convenient for you to use.

Potential re-binds that may benefit you are Interact: Right-Mouse Button (F key by default) and Inventory: Tab key (I key by default).
