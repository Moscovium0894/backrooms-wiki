Achievements

Achievements are unlockable badges through steam by completing certain tasks. In Escape the Backrooms, there are 34 obtainable Achievements, most of which are for escaping levels. Achievements can also be reset through the settings menu in the game category.

Trivia

  • The game's Release had 12 Achievements, Part 2 had 20 Achievements, Part 3 had 25 Achievements, Part 4 had 30 Achievements, and Version 1.0 has 34 Achievements.
  • The icons of the difficulty Achievements (excluding the Normal one "Squad") originate from certain places instead of just a render there for the Achievement.
    • The "Participation Trophy" Achievement icon originates from Fancy's 3rd Devlog when he was talking about the Survival gamemode.
    • The "Group Effort" Achievement icon originates from the game's banner artwork on Steam.
    • The "Nightmare" Achievement icon originates from one of the game's Steam page screenshots, which is a render of something seen in Fancy's 3rd Devlog when he was talking about the Survival gamemode.
  • Previously, the "Escape Level 52" Achievement had "Turn on screen reader support" in its description for some reason, which made the description "Find the exit in Level 52. Turn on screen reader support". It is unknown why this was the case, but it has since then been fixed.
  • Five levels do not have completion Achievements. These levels are Level 0.11, Level 37.2, Level Fun+, the Bunker, and Level you Cheated.
    • Level 0.11 does not have an Achievement because it was initially going to be the rest of the Level 188 Courtyard at the end of the level. Fancy eventually made Level 0.11 the level instead without changing the Level 188 Courtyard area and seemingly never changed the Achievement.
    • It is unknown why Level 37.2 and Level Fun+ do not have completion Achievements.
    • The Bunker likely does not have a completion Achievement due to its main purpose being to get the player or players to Overgrowth and to show the location of the secret levels, which makes the level extremely short.
    • Level You Cheated likely does not have a completion Achievement because you can't even complete the level, and it's supposed to remain hidden in all parts of the game it seems.
  • Level 1 and Level 2's completion Achievements call the levels by their sub-names in the Achievement names, unlike every other Achievement which uses "Level ?" if the level has a level number.
  • Overgrowth's completion Achievement calls the level the Death Slides instead of Overgrowth.
  • Only one Achievement name has an exclamation mark at the end of it, which is the "Escaped!" Achievement.
  • Majority of Achievement descriptions have punctuation at the end of their descriptions except for three.
    • These three include "Escape Level 4", "Escape the Death Slides", and "Escaped!". "Escape Level !" could also be considered one because the exclamation mark at the end of its description is apart of the level's sub-name, however an exclamation mark is also a punctuation mark and putting a period after an exclamation mark wouldn't make sense.
  • Only one Achievement description uses an exclamation mark at the end of its description instead of a period, which is the "Escape Level !" Achievement.
  • There were a few differences to the Achievements before Part 2, those being:
    • The "Survivalist" Achievement was obtained by surviving in Level 0 for 10 minutes instead of 15.
    • The "Participation Trophy" Achievement was misspelled and was titled "Participation Tropy".
    • The "Group Effort" Achievement was obtained for completing the game in Multiplayer in Normal instead of Hard.
  • Version 1.0 brought a few changes to the Achievements, those being:
    • All the level escape Achievements mentioned escaping the levels in Singleplayer were changed because of Singleplayer being changed to not be its own thing.
    • Descriptions of Level 0, Level Fun, Level !, and The End's completion Achievement were had slightly different wording as opposed to other level escape Achievements, which were changed to match the rest in Version 1.0. The descriptions said "Escape (level name here) in Singleplayer." instead of "Find the exit in (level name here)." Level 0's Achievement also said complete instead of escape. The "Survivalist" Achievement also said "in Singleplayer" at the end of it. For some reason, Level 2's completion Achievement still does something similar and mentions finding the exit door instead of just the exit.
  • The only time Achievements have ever been added or changed is during major updates.
