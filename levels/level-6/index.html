Appearance

Level 6 is a pitch-black maze of darkness. When using a Flashlight, it shows the Level to be a destroyed, abandoned, and dirty labyrinth. You can find rusty lockers, dirty tables with Items on them, ladders, coils, and more objects scattered throughout the level.

Objective

Part 1: The Flashlight Area

When you first spawn in, you will find yourself in a dark room with the only light coming from 2 rooms on the side and an entrance to the rest of the level. The left room has a ton of Almond Water, Juice, and Energy Bars, while the right room has 4 Flashlights and some items on the ground. Both rooms have windows and are 2 of the only four rooms to have some sort of light. When you enter the rest of the level, you will need to use a Flashlight to navigate the flashlight area. There are also some rooms here that contain items you can find. Look for a room that has a flickering light to move on.

Part 2: The Maze

Once you find the room, you will also find a hallway that continues on to a pitch-black labyrinth where Flashlights don't work. You need to use a LiDAR Scanner from the flickering light room on the left to shoot dots onto the walls and navigate the maze. LiDAR Scanners also have trackers so you can see where the Wretch roaming the level is. Find a short hallway with a light coming through a door to get to Level 7.

Entrances & Exits

Entrances

  • Get on the Roller Coaster after surviving 100 seconds (1 minute and 40 seconds) in the Animated King boss battle in Level 94 to get here.

Exits

  • Navigate through the labyrinth of Level 6 and find the entrance to Level 7 to escape.
  • If you've discovered the Wrong Door Ending, use an ID Card on the M.E.G. secured door right next to the Level 7 entrance and navigate through the smaller maze to find the entrance to Level 974.

Trivia

  • There are three places where natural light occurs: the beginning, where light from a midnight sky shoots through the windows, the flickering light room, and the exit, which comes from the door that leads to Level 7.
  • In Fancy's 1st Devlog, he said that making the Backrooms levels in order would get repetitive and a lot of the levels are pretty boring, mentioning Level 6, Level 7, and Level 10. Ironically though, all three of these levels have been added to the game.
  • In Fancy's 3rd Devlog, it was mentioned that this level was originally going to have a mechanic where you would have to listen to sounds to escape, but this for some reason got replaced by the LiDAR Scanner.
  • It was hard for Fancy to find references on what the level looks like due to nearly every image of the level just being a black screen. When he did find a reference image, it was designed into the room at the start of the level.
  • The LiDAR Scanner was added because roaming around the level with no idea where you are and where to go would make a pretty annoying level.
  • Just like Level 94 and Level 0.11, the main render of this level was used on Level 6's page on the Backrooms Fandom wiki. The picture originates from this wiki and as a result is credited to it.
