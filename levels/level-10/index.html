Appearance

Level 10 is a very large and extensive wheat field. It has windmills scattered around the level and a barn. There are lakes in the level which act as landmarks. The majority of the level is wheat and fences, with a patch of corn next to the barn.

Objective

Part 1: Stalker in the Road

Upon spawning in, you'll be on a road. Simply walk down the road until you arrive at a barn, but the walk won't be easy. Every now and then, be sure to look back and make sure the Stalker Faceling isn't sneaking up on your team. Upon spotting it, it will return into the fields. If it gets to whoever it targets, it will take them into the wheat fields never to be seen again.

Part 2: Lighting the way out

Upon arrival to the barn, go through it, and you'll arrive at what seems to be the end. An opening that leads to the wheat, but you can actually walk through the wheat. Scattered throughout the tall wheat field is 5 Windmills the next one to enable having a red light shining through the fog to guide wanderers to it. These red lights will become green upon finding the Windmill and interacting with the generator inside of it, which will enable the red light of the next Windmill. After activating the first Windmill and heading to the next wheat opening, a Faceling with a Chainsaw will come out of it and start the Chainsaw. After this, the Farmer Faceling will always be hunting you down but will spawn at a different place closer to you or your team depending on what checkpoint the level is loaded from, and the only way to avoid it is by running away. To easily survive traversing the tall wheat, it's best to stick to fences so you can easily get to openings for your first few playthroughs. Enable all the Windmills until arriving at the fifth Windmill, which is beside the parking lot of Level 3999 and is not required to enable since you've already arrived at the Arcade. Enter the Arcade to get to Level 3999.

Trivia

  • This level is very disliked due its tedious nature.
  • You can skip to the end of this level by crouch jumping from a haystack onto the fence, which will allow you to walk on top of the fence all the way to the Arcade.
  • This level in the first teaser was shown to have seemingly a blue sky, making it feel safe. But in the final version, the level has dark, grey fog that completely hides the original nice blue sky, making it feel not so safe anymore.
  • Level 10 was first revealed in August of 2022, and released on June 15th, 2023 in Part 3 almost a year after its existence in the game was first revealed, making it the Level that has taken the second most time to make. The level that took the longest time to make is Level 974.
  • In Hotfix #14, an extra Windmill was added to the level.
  • The layout of this level was originally different, but it was slightly changed to how it is now in Hotfix #15.
  • Originally, this level was apparently just supposed to be the road at the start of the level, but it became much more after Fancy read further into the Backrooms Fandom Wiki page for this level.
  • In Fancy's 1st Devlog, he said that making the Backrooms levels in order would get repetitive and a lot of the levels are pretty boring, mentioning Level 6, Level 7, and Level 10. Ironically though all three of these levels have still ended up in the game.
  • Fancy considered this level as a bad level because of the level being really boring, but important to lore. He also said in his 8th Devlog that he'll never add a bad level again after hearing the response to Level 10.
