Wanderer Friendly

Wanderers are humans that are trapped in the Backrooms who are always seen wearing a hazmat suit, the design of which can be changed in the Character Select screen. All Wanderers are about 1.8m tall (~5'10.87ft). In the game, you play as a Wanderer, whom the identity of is currently unknown.

Appearance

Main Level

Level 0 resembles an office from around the 1970s with a bright yellow wallpaper and a damp, unkept carpet. The level is quite average sized and has two layers.

The Smaller Area

if you are entering this level from Level 8, you will find yourself in a separate and much smaller area of the level. There is a room in this area as well known as The Manilla Room, and there are boards that block off Level 0.11 in The Manilla Room. The Hub Door can also be found in this area as well. There is also a small part of Level 0.2 you can find close by. To get back to the rest of Level 0 from this area, in The Manilla Room, you must have a Crowbar to break a Vent. This Vent will bring you back to the rest of the level.

Objective

New game

This is the objective for starting a new game.

Part 1: Fixing the Ladder

Upon starting a new game, a cutscene plays where the player is with another Wanderer in a hallway. The Wanderer asks the player, "Mind uh, checking for anyone's still vacuuming that backroom?", and the player enters the backroom. After walking for a short while, they find that the door they entered through is gone, and the game begins. Upon spawning in, you will either find yourself in a room with a bunch of pillars, or an area that's right next to the pillar room. Both of these spawn points have a Note on a wall nearby. You need to look around the level until you find a room with a broken Ladder. Collect all 4 of the ladder pieces to fix it, and then a cutscene will play of the player climbing up the Ladder to get to a small box at the top and obtain a Key. Once the Key is obtained, head left of the Ladder until you find a wooden door. Unlock the door to enter the Pitfalls.

Part 2: The Pitfalls

The locked door leads to a room that is different compared to the rest of the level. It contains a set of walkways and pits called the Pitfalls, and on the other side of the room is the exit. You must balance yourself across them using Q and E and make it to the other side. On the other side, there is some Rope (that can be used to retrieve anyone else who has fallen down) and a Vent that leads to Level 1. Failing to balance yourself will lead to you falling in the holes, leading to the second layer. The second layer is similar to the rest of Level 0, though being much darker and having much more open space. An alternate exit can be found by following arrows drawn on the wall. After following the arrows for some time, you will find a room that looks completely different from the rest of the level. It looks to be a normal-ish house, featuring a kitchen that has blue lights, a completely dark room that only has a drawer with a Flashlight on top, and stairs that lead to a hallway with a Vent. Entering the Vent will send you to Level 1.

From Level 8

This is the objective for entering from Level 8.

Part 1: Figuring out the Locker Code

Once you enter the level from Level 8, you will be in the smaller area. In the miniature Level 0.2, there is a Light Switch on the wall. Use it and the lights will go out for a short time. when this happens, over a number on the wall, a shape will appear over a certain one. This is the code for a Locker in The Manilla Room. The only difference about this code is of course, it uses shapes. The numbers represent the order you put in the code, and the shapes represent what that button will be. Every single time you press the light switch, a shape will appear over the next number.

Part 2: Entering Level 0.11

If you enter the Locker code correctly, the Locker will open, and there will be a Chainsaw inside. If you forget or need the code for any other reason it's Square, Circle, Arrow. Use this Chainsaw to break down the boards that lead to Level 0.11, and once done, enter the small Level 0.11 room on the other side to proceed on. When in the entrance to the Level for a short time, you will noclip through the ground and find yourself in the Level.

Entrances & Exits

Entrances

  • Start a new game to get here.
  • Climb up the Ladder at the end of Level 8 to get to the smaller area.

Exits

  • Balance yourself across the Pitfalls and enter the Vent on the other side of the room to get to Level 1.
  • Go up the stairs in the house in the second layer and enter the Vent to get to Level 1.
  • Use the Chainsaw from the Locker to break down the boards in The Manilla Room and enter the area to get to Level 0.11.
  • When entering from Level 8, go to the room on the left and enter The Hub Door to get to The Hub.

The Hub Door

To find The Hub Door in this level, you must come here from Level 8 because it's located in the smaller area. If you’re in The Manilla Room, travel toward Level 0.2's area, then slightly right until you can’t go more forward. Around, you should be able to find the Door.

Release Version

This level was one that was added on the game's Release. The level had the same layout, just with checkerboard Pitfalls, and there was also no second layer. The Ladder room had the Ladder already fixed, and the exit could have spawned in either the broken ladder room, or on the other side of the Pitfalls which displayed the Ladder instead of a vent. All you would have to do is touch the Ladder (even though the box led up to nothing) and then you could move onto Level 1.

Trivia

  • In the second exit after you fall down in the Pitfalls, there is a Flashlight on a table in the dark room beside the staircase. This is one of the only Items you can find in Level 0, with the other Item being the Rope.
  • The Pitfalls were originally completely different, and instead of narrow pathways, they were in a checkerboard pattern. If you fell, you would get sent to a completely random area of the Level.
  • Level 0 has the most amount of Sublevels related to it out of any other level in the entire game, and the only one to have more than one.
  • In order to model the level, Fancy just copied and pasted a bunch of cubes around and added textures. He also mentioned how making the Backrooms is really easy because of this.
  • This level was originally going to be the same room over and over again infinitely. This was the original procedural generation system.
  • The original procedural generation system was implemented because of Level 0's size and so Fancy didn't have to copy and paste the Level for hours.
  • In the original Backrooms lore, Level 0 is completely devoid of entities. However, Fancy added the Bacteria to it to add some gameplay.
  • Before the game was released, the walls had another different texture that looked much different. Fancy mentioned that he changed the texture in his third Devlog.
  • In Fancy's 9th Devlog, he showed this level with Noclipping Floors and mentioned how he added them to a few levels, which might be hinting at Noclipping Floors coming to this level and others in the future.
  • There is another promotional image of the level, which is assumed to be the main level picture but at a different angle.
  • There was once a bug in the Pitfalls where players can walk over the entire Pitfalls in milliseconds, this was used by speedrunners.
  • There's a bug where you can't fall into the Pitfalls. To do this, you need to go to the edge of the Pitfalls and then look at the walls.
