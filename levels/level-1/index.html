Threat assessment
Skin-Stealer Deadly

Skin-Stealers are humanoids with a mouth that goes down to their body. Its skin is gray, and it has 2 glowing white eyes. There are also various blood spots on it mainly around its mouth. Skin-Stealers possess the unique ability to steal a player's skin after killing them and disguise itself as a wanderer.

FIELD TIP — Run away from any Players that appear to be chasing you.

Smiler Moderate

Smilers are floating smiling faces with sharp teeth with dark shadowy fog surrounding their bodies. Smilers are mysterious and shadow-like entities that are typically seen in pitch black places where only their smiling faces can be seen.

FIELD TIP — Try to stay in the light.

Ghost Smiler Minimal

The Ghost Smiler is an entity only appearing in Level 1 . It does not possess any physical form, only appearing in an event which results in the player's immediate death. During that appearance it resembles a regular smiler .

FIELD TIP — Stay in the main floor in level 1 at all costs.

Almond Water Flashlight

Appearance

Level 1 is a massive parking garage made of concrete with office rooms and a lot of Almond Water. This is also the first level where Sanity will drain.

Objective

Part 1: Unlock the Elevator

Once you first spawn in, you will find an Elevator with a Color-Coded Lock that will send you to floor 2, but you need a code to access it. You will need to explore the level to find a red door that leads to a room with 4 different colored cars. The colors of the cars are the code, so remember the colors so you can enter the code in the elevator to enter the next area. For example, if 1 car is blue, 2 cars are red and 1 car is green, the code is 1 blue button, 2 red buttons and 1 green button. Inside the office rooms around the level, you can find useful Items such as Almond Water and Flashlights. Use Almond Water to restore your Sanity, because it will begin to drain in this floor.

Part 2: Get the Keys

Floor 2 is just like the first one, but much bigger. In this floor, you need to explore the rooms in order to find 4 Keys. The Keys can be found on desks, closets, and open drawers. You can also find Almond Water and Flashlights in this floor just like the first one. There is also a Skin-Stealer in this floor (2 if you are playing in Multiplayer with 3 or more Players), so be careful. After you find all 4 of the Keys, look around for a room that has a vault door. The vault door leads to floor 3, however it needs the Keys to open. Insert the 4 Keys into 4 different Keyholes that are around the room and have them all turned at the same time. They will unturn over time, so you have to be quick. If they're all turned at the same time, the vault door will open, allowing you to move onto floor 3.

Part 3: Find the Elevator

Floor 3 is a dimly-lit-area that has 3 individual floors. All of the floors are the exact same thing, except each one has a slight layout change. At the start, you'll be in a room made out of bricks, and it has an opening to stairs. Go down the stairs, and you'll find a more normal looking room with shelves containing Flashlights and Almond Water. Open the door, and you'll find the main part of the Level. To move on, look around for an Elevator that will bring you to floor 4. There is one possible Elevator spawn location for each floor, and the floor the Elevator is on is random. If you find a locked door, that means that's the door the Elevator can spawn on in the floor you're on, but it didn't. You're not alone in this floor however, because there is a Skin-Stealer roaming around. There's also only Items at the start of the floor, so make sure to use them wisely.

Part 4: Unlock the Gate

Floor 4 is a small room with much rougher concrete walls, and a closed gate. You must get through this gate in order to escape, but security override is needed to flip the switch to open the gate. In order to get access to flip the switch, you will need to go to The Hub. The Hub Door will be on the left side of the room and should be fairly simple to find. Once you enter, use the Computers in The M.E.G. Base to get access to the switch and then return to the Level.

Part 5: STAY iN THE LiGHT

Once the gate is open, you'll find an area resembling a parking garage. There are a few small hallways which the Player will have to run toward to survive all the Smilers when the lights turn off, and they contain two blue Double Doors which can either lead to a small empty room, a small room with a few Office Drawers (which can contain Flashlights and Almond Water), or the entrance to Level 2 (which will be random each time). After the lights switch back on, it will be safe to proceed to the next hallway. It's recommended you only go to one hallway at a time, because the lights only stay on for 5-10 seconds, and very rarely stay on for up to 20 seconds. There is also a ramp leading to a second floor in this floor, but it's highly recommended not to go to unless you've discovered the Wrong Door Ending, because that would allow you to use an ID Card on a M.E.G. sealed door to get to Level 9223372036854775807.

Entrances & Exits

Entrances

  • Cross the Pitfalls in Level 0 and go through the Vent on the other side to get to Level 1.
  • Going up the stairs in the liminal house below the Pitfalls in Level 0 will lead to Level 1.
  • Returning through the doors in Level 9223372036854775807.

Exits

  • In the parking garage in Floor 4, find the hallway that resembles Level 2 to get to Level 2.
  • In Floor 4, go through the door with the fire exit sign to get to The Hub.
  • On the upper area of Floor 4, if you've discovered the Wrong Door Ending, use an ID Card on a M.E.G. secured door and enter it to get to Level 9223372036854775807.

The Hub Door

In Floor 4, the Door will be out in the open, and it's the first Level Door in The Hub that can be unlocked. The Door is required to unlock to progress.

Release Version

This level was one that was added on the Game's Release. The level only included the garage in floor 4, and the Doors didn't contain Drawers because the Inventory wasn't added yet. The only other difference the level had was that if you went up the ramp, the Ghost Smiler would kill you.

Trivia

  • If you use a Flashlight on the darkness of the Level 2 entrance, you'll see that the hallway stops at a close wall rather than displaying Level 2's true geometry,
  • The ambience of Floor 4 is called "Level1_Ambience" in the game files, but the Floor 1 ambience is called "Level1_Ambience_2" despite being the first floor and Floor 4 being the fourth. The reason for this is likely because Floor 4 was the first to be added to the game.
  • In Fancy's first Devlog, he showed the garage part of Floor 4 without the hallways.
  • When Fancy mentioned what entities Level 1 has, he mentioned Skin-Stealers (which were later added to the level), Scratchers, Facelings and Hounds but only made the Scratcher but didn't add it to the level when the game released.
