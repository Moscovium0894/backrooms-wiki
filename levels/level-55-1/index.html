Smiler Moderate

Smilers are floating smiling faces with sharp teeth with dark shadowy fog surrounding their bodies. Smilers are mysterious and shadow-like entities that are typically seen in pitch black places where only their smiling faces can be seen.

FIELD TIP — Try to stay in the light.

Skin-Stealer Deadly

Skin-Stealers are humanoids with a mouth that goes down to their body. Its skin is gray, and it has 2 glowing white eyes. There are also various blood spots on it mainly around its mouth. Appearance

Level 55.1

Level 55.1 is a large room with a massive vertical tunnel in the center, and an elevator is connected to the tunnel that can be used to access the various layers. The tunnel has three areas that you can access: Level 0.2, Level 0.3, and Level 0.11. However, these are smaller versions of their respective levels aside from Level 0.2 and Level 0.3.

Level 0.2

Level 0.2 resembles Level 0 but with white walls and red carpet.

Level 0.3

Level 0.3 resembles Level 0 but with a blue hue present everywhere in the area. The Bacteria roams around Level 0.3.

Level 0.11

Level 0.11 resembles Level 0 but everything is damaged, and the majority of the place is dark. Unlike the original Level 0.11, Level 0.11 has Smilers and a Skin-Stealer roaming around. There are also two groups of 3 inanimate Moai Statues you can find.

Objective

Part 1: Photography left off

Upon spawning in, you might want to pick up some items on the tables of the first hallway, they might be useful. Then, head to the tunnel itself and pick up a Camera from the table next to the 6 computers. On the Camera, you'll see a photo attached to it. To escape the level, you and your teammates must travel to the locations using the elevator and use the Camera to take pictures of the photos.

The Photos Include:

  • If you're playing alone, the first photo is of a message spray painted on one of the walls reading "Who is here?" in Level 0.2. If you're not playing alone, it will be an image of 1-3 players depending on how many people you're playing with.
  • A construction area in Level 0.2.
  • A rundown van in Level 0.3.
  • The Bacteria in Level 0.3.
  • The Moai Statues in Level 0.11.
  • The Skin-Stealer in Level 0.11 (disguised or not).

Part 2: Overgrown

After everything has been photographed, the screen on all the Cameras will turn white and the iron gate in the ceiling will open. Take the elevator up through the gate instead of deeper into the tunnel, and upon reaching the top, a new area will be accessible. In there, there's a room that looks like an overgrown version of Level 0. Most importantly, the room has 2 doors at the end. On the other side of the doors, there is a white void. Jumping in the white void will give you the Wrong Door Ending.

Upon jumping into the white void, a cutscene will play of the camera falling from the sky. The camera will fall into a road, where a car drives up and runs it over. The player's fate is unconfirmed, but it can be speculated that the player died due to the fall.

Trivia

  • This is an original Sublevel custom-made just for the game.
  • This is the only Sublevel whose parent level isn't in the game.
  • The ambience of this Level is reused from the lights out ambience in The End.
  • The appearance of this Level originates from a Streetview on Google Earth, which appeared as the large tunnel with Level 0 nearby it. The location of it is "Fort Campbell" and it is now removed, however The Hub Mode description mentions how the level first appeared on a satellite image referencing the Google Earth appearance of the level. The idea of the Level containing multiple Sublevels traversable through the use of an Elevator is an original idea.
  • Despite the video where this level was revealed, it doesn't appear on the backrooms wikidot.
  • In the game files, this Level is called "Tunnel Level".
  • The Moai Statues are a reference to the removed Moai Statue Entities in Level 0.11.
  • This Level was originally shown to have a view of Level 0 at the top floor similar to the original image of this Level, but this for some reason got removed.
    • This could have been due to the Level not being connected to Level 0, but instead Level 52.
  • The lever to control the Elevator was originally planned to be separate from the elevator, meaning that you would have to have somebody at the top controlling the elevator, but this would have made the Level impossible to complete in Singleplayer, so the Lever got moved to the elevator for both Singleplayer and Multiplayer. It is unknown why this wasn't kept for Multiplayer, but it might return.
  • If you use a Flashlight to see the dark areas of the top floor, you will find a wall texture similar to the Level 0 one.
  • This is the only Level in the game to have fall damage.
