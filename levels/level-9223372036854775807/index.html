Appearance

Level 9223372036854775807 is an infinite stone staircase with openings where a white void can be seen. The stairs are sorted out through floors, and each floor has a number corresponding to its floor number. It has been theorized that this is the deepest level in the Backrooms.

Objective

Once you first spawn in, you will be at the starting floor. The stairs will lead to the different floors, and every floor is labelled from 0-99. To escape, you must travel up the staircase and look at the number labels on each floor. If one of the numbers is incorrectly labelled 94, jump down the center of the staircase and once you reach the bottom, you'll fall through a portal and get The Suburbs Ending.

Trivia

  • The idea of going from Level 1 of the Backrooms to Level 9223372036854775807 of the Backrooms is pretty ironic.
  • The level number (9223372036854775807) is the maximum 64-bit signed integer limit on a computer.
  • This level has the longest level number, with a total of 19 numbers in the level number.
  • The level number is commonly shortened to just 922 when mentioned in some parts of the game. This has especially happened a lot after Version 1.0.
    • 922 is now the level's new official number on the Backrooms Fandom wiki where it originates due to new rules enacted to fill in the first cluster of 1,001 levels before moving onto the next.
  • This is the only procedurally generated level in the game.
  • The floor that the incorrectly labelled 94 will be at depends on the difficulty. On Easy it's somewhere on floors 10-30. For Normal and Hard, it's somewhere on floors 20-50. And in Nightmare, it will be somewhere on floors 10-the first ∞ floor.
  • If you travel beyond floor 99, every floor after that will just be labelled "∞" and the floors will keep on going forever.
  • Originally, this level was accessed between The End and Level 94 and was apart of the main level path, but in Version 1.0, this was changed. Level 9223372036854775807 is currently the only level to have went from a main path level to a secret level.
    • Because of this, the level used to have a M.E.G. Base Entry, but it was removed for becoming a secret level. The description of it was "Level 9223372036854775807 is an incredibly tall, dark, spiral staircase surrounded by walls, and is currently undetermined in size. nobody has been able to reach the top, so it leads many to believe that it's infinite.", It was listed as "Class 0 - No Danger", its Sanity Danger was "Class 0 - No Sanity Risk", its file name was "Level 92235...", and it would have shown your fastest escape.
  • If you have a save file at Level 9223372036854775807 before Version 1.0, update your game and load the save file, and complete Level 9223372036854775807, you will get The Suburbs Ending like normal. Afterwards, the game will think that you have unlocked both the Wrong Door Ending and The Suburbs Ending in the save file.
  • This level originally had a completely different design, and Fancy decided to redo it to make it look more realistic.
  • On the Backrooms Fandom Wiki where this level originates, it is now known as Level 922.
  • In the original Backrooms lore, jumping from the 5,000th floor would let you escape into reality, however, in the game this is not true.
