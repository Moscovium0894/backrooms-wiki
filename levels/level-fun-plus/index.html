Threat assessment
Partygoer Dangerous

Partygoers are yellow humanoid creatures with blood being easily noticeable on the mouth, hands, and legs. They hold Balloons and they stay in the same position, but if they spot you they will start chasing you. In Level Fun+ , they walk around the place and don't just stand in one place.

FIELD TIP — Crouch when trying to get around one.

Party Host Indirectly Deadly

Party Hosts are cakes with bright green icing with an Eyeball in the middle. The color of the eye is blue. Party Hosts are immobile and will always be on the wall or the ceiling.

FIELD TIP — Throw a Firework directly at them from a safe distance to kill them and not kill yourself from the blast.

Partypooper Friendly

Partypoopers are friendly humanoid entities that wear black jeans, a black hoodie, and black gloves. They also wear a blue mask with a sad face on it, but their actual face can never be seen by normal means. Majority of Partypoopers are dead after the events of The Fun War and only one is still seen alive in-game.

FIELD TIP — Follow the Partypooper's directions.

Appearance

Level Fun+ is an open party area with many plastic Tables and chairs present throughout the level. Unlike Level Fun, this level has different colored walls, tons of areas, and more open ones. The floor is covered by a carpet made for little kids, and there are 4 sectors that you must go to in order to escape. The Level is also the home of the leader of the Partygoers: The Party Host.

Objective

Part 1: The Party Room

Once you first spawn in, you will be in a ball pit and connected to it will be the Party Room. The Party Room contains a bunch of Tables with chairs along with 4 different colored hallways. Each hallway leads to a different sector. In every sector, there will be a key that you must obtain to escape. The order of the sectors you go into doesn't matter as you just need all of the keys.

Part 2: Getting Used to the Sectors

The sectors you can find include:

  • The Party Sector (the bright blue hallway with clouds on the wall)
  • The Playhouse Sector (the pink hallway with clouds on the wall)
  • The Arcade Sector (the red hallway)
  • The Funhouse Sector (the dark blue hallway with plastic balls)
  • The Party Sector
  • The Playhouse Sector
  • The Arcade Sector
  • The Funhouse Sector
The Party Sector is a massive area with a bunch of walls, Tables, and a large room in the middle containing tables, chairs, and a different wall design. Your goal here is to go around and pop all of the Balloons scattered around because one of them will contain the yellow key. However, the Balloon the yellow key will be in a different room each time. The sector has 3 Partygoers roaming around, so keep an eye out for them. The Playhouse Sector starts off with rooms similar to the original Level Fun, just much smaller. In here you can find a big square room with Balloons everywhere, and a hallway that connects to 3 rooms. These rooms look just like nostalgic houses, and you must find a Tape in one of the 3 rooms. The Tapes only have 1 spawn for each room, so it shouldn't be too hard to find. Once you have the Tape, go back to the room with the Balloons and insert the Tape into the TV. The video shows a Balloon icon, and then the color green will flash on the screen. You must then simply pop a green Balloon. Then, it will do it again, but after green it will show yellow. Then, you have to pop a green and then yellow Balloon. The next color it will show is blue. And then it will show Red. And lastly, pink. If you get a color wrong, all of the Balloons will respawn and you will have to start over. Once all of the Balloons are popped, the secured vault door will open, and inside is a room very similar to the original photo of Level Fun. There is a wooden Table in this room as well as artwork of Partygoers and Partypoopers. On the wooden table is the green key. The Arcade Sector is a neon-type area with an area that features a bunch of golf courses, and also some tables that contain Glowsticks as well as a Bowling Alley. In the Golf Area, there are 4 Glowstick tables, and each one has a different color glowstick. The colors include green, blue, red, and yellow. You will need one of every color as well as an extra one. Once you've collected all of the Glowsticks you need, go up the stairs and enter the dark zone. Get out one of the Glowsticks to brighten up the place, as Flashlights do not work in the Zone, and then you must go around the darkness and find 4 different symbols. 3 of the symbols are on the walls in the halls, and one of them is in a closet in one of the rooms. The symbols include balloons, a present, an eye, and a rainbow. The rainbow is the one stored in the closet. Once you find a symbol, you must place down the correct Glowstick right below it. When you are holding the correct Glowstick, the Symbol will light up. The Balloons need a green Glowstick, the present will need a red one, the eye will need a yellow one, and the rainbow will need a blue one. There is a Partygoer roaming around the dark zone, so keep an eye out for it. Once all of the Glowsticks are placed down, you can leave the dark zone and go to the locked door, and it will be open. Inside will be the blue key.

The Funhouse Sector is a cluster of rooms with different fun children's activities in them like trampolines, a rock-climbing wall, and more. The room you start off in is fairly normal and appears to be a school classroom. There's a locked blue door and steel gray doors that have bars around them, and on the roof of this classroom lies a Party Host. In order to move on, you must kill it. In order to kill it, grab a Firework from a table and throw it at the Party Host. Then, the blue door will be unlocked. You will then find yourself in a much bigger room with a wooden pathway as well as other segments. There are more steel doors, but there are no longer bars around them. The next Party Host is also above the doors. Every now and then, Partygoers will come out of the doors, or if the Party Host spots you they'll come out so killing the Party Hosts now won't be that easy. Once you kill the Party Host, the next room has a rock-climbing wall in the middle, and the Party Host is on the top right of the room. Once again, beware of the Partygoers. Once you kill the Party Host, you must go into the room on the right side of the room first.

This room has the Party Host above a box with a bunch of presents, and letters reading "HAPPY BIRTHDAY". There are no steel doors in this room, however the Partygoers will still come from the previous room. After killing the Party Host, you can go into the room on the left side. This room is a ball pit with balloons on the wall. There are also pillars, and there actually is a steel door. The Party Host in this room is on the ceiling. Kill it, and then you can move onto the next room. The doors to proceed on will be at the top of the room, and this next area is the hardest in this sector. It features a pathway to walk on and a ball pit. The pathway will lead to the Party Host right above the steel door. After killing the Party Host, you can go up a slope that leads down into an area that doesn't have a ball pit, but does have a trampoline and structural support. The Party Host here is quite high, because it's on the ceiling. Make sure to keep an eye on the steel door as well. Kill the Party Host, and a room on the bottom right of this area will unlock that has a ball pit, a slide to go down into the ball pit, and the red key. Collecting the Red Key will make the Sector safe to traverse.

Part 3: Opening the Gate

Once you have all 4 of the Keys, you can go back to the main area and go up to a Key Machine. In the Key Machine, you must move around the thing in the Machine and go to the Keyhole that corresponds with the color of the Key it has out. Once that is done, insert the key into the hole, and another key will pop out. The previous one will fall down. Now, you must go to the keyhole that corresponds with that key. Then, the key will be swapped out again. And then again. However, after using the last key, the exit gate will open, and you can get to Level 52.

Entrances & Exits

Entrances

Go up the stairs in the big room in Level 37.2 and go down any of the slides to get here.

Exits

Collect all 4 Keys and insert them into the Key Machine. Then, the gate will open, and you can get to Level 52.

Trivia

The Sublevel

  • This level has the most Note variations in the entire game with a total of 5.
  • For some reason, this level does not have an Achievement for completing it or a Hub Entry.

In-Game Information

Details

  • The wall design of the room with the blue key is the same as the wall texture of the room behind the locked blue door/the old Arcade room at the end of Level Fun.

Community

  • This level won the Backrooms Bracket, an event hosted by the game's publisher Secret Mode on the Escape the Backrooms Discord server to determine which level is the best in the whole game.

Media & References

  • The billiard room in the Arcade Sector might be a reference to Buckshot Roulette, as they are both on the second floor of a party type setting and feature a billiard board in the room. Buckshot Roulette was also released before Part 4 was, which makes it more likely.

Game Files

  • The Note in the Party Sector was found in the game files of Part 2 and was most likely going to have been used for the locked door at the end of Level Fun, although in Part 4, it was moved to Level Fun+.

Production Facts

  • This level was added because of how small Level Fun is.
  • The Partypoopers were most likely originally going to appear in this level but were moved to Level 52.

Sublevel Inspiration

  • This is the only level in the game from the Backrooms Freewriting Wiki.
