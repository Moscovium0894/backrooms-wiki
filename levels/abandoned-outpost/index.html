Abandoned Outpost is a base in Escape the Backrooms. It appears to be a lab where Wretches were studied by M.E.G.

Mission

Getting into the base

Completing Abandoned Outpost is a requirement for escaping Darkened Suburbs. To get into the base, in Darkened Suburbs, players must enable three Computers found in the houses of the Level, which opens the gate to the large house with the base inside. The base is located inside the house, and in order to find the base, look for the Door with a spray-painted arrow pointing at it. On the other side is the basement to the house which leads to the base, and once a player (or all the players if in Nightmare) walks into the basement, it will send everyone in the lobby to the base.

Part 1: Figuring out the mission

Upon entry, simply head forward and go through the two hallways that lead into a room with an Emergency Platform and a projector. Once someone in the lobby enters the room, the projector will automatically play a video explaining how to turn a Wretch back to normal. The rest of the base can't be explored until the video is done because the M.E.G. Door leading to the rest of the base remains automatically closed until the video finishes. In the room is a Note on the table with the Filtering Unit that mentions a Wretch in the base that has an Administrator Card that can get one anywhere, hinting at what the mission is.

Part 2: The Concentrate

Once the video ends, the M.E.G. door to the rest of the base will automatically open and remain open forever, allowing exploration of the entire base. At this point, everyone must find 4 bottles of Almond Concentrate and put them into the filtering unit in the projector room. Don't get too comfortable in this base because a Wretch with a Crowbar is roaming it, who carries the needed administrator card. If it spots a player, they can hide in a locker, and it will go away after a short amount of time. Or they can hide inside one of the 2 Vents found in the base but be sure they are opened with a crowbar. Keep in mind that three crowbars can be found in the base, so it's a better idea to hide in a locker, considering there are 16 within the base. Once all 4 bottles of concentrate are collected, make sure they are put into the filtering unit. To put one in, a player can select an Inventory slot that has the concentrate in hand, then press the interact key while looking at the filtering unit. Each red light below the bottles will turn green once each of the almond concentrate are put in place. This will enable the Emergency Safety Shower to function.

Part 3: No more Wretches here

Once the emergency safety shower is ready, everyone will need to get the Wretch in the emergency platform to use it. Just find a way to lure the Wretch to the projector room, and onto the platform standing point. Be sure when luring the Wretch onto the platform that you or another teammate is standing right behind it by the M.E.G. door that leads out of the base. Once it stands on this point, the platform will automatically close trapping the Wretch inside. Now anyone can push the emergency safety shower until it can't be pushed anymore. Turn it on, and the Wretch will be sprayed with the substance from the emergency safety shower that comes from the almond concentrate, which reverts it back into a human Wanderer, making it safe to collect their card. The emergency safety shower also can't be turned on until it's fully pushed. Once the card is collected, the base will no longer be important, and everyone can leave it to finish and escape Darkened Suburbs.

Projector Video Transcript

"Knowing how to deal with emergencies can greatly reduce the risk of an accidental contamination event. Whenever you enter the laboratory, be sure to locate the emergency safety shower, as it may have been moved to a different area. In the event of an accidental contamination, instruct the affected person to stand in the middle of the emergency platform on top of the footprints. The platform should automatically close to ensure everyone's safety. Next, make sure the filtering unit contains 4 full bottles of concentrate. If some of the bottles are only partially filled or missing, place a new bottle in its spot. You'll know that the unit is ready when all 4 lights are green. Now you can push the emergency shower forward until it's locked in place and can't be moved anymore. At this point, you can rotate the handle on the shower to activate it. The platform should remain closed and will automatically open up when the process is complete. Manually opening the platform can cause harm to the affected person or others in the laboratory. Please make sure to clean the equipment and replace all the materials before leaving."

Trivia

The Base

  • Based on the projector video, the canon name of the base seems to be "The Laboratory".
  • Abandoned Outpost is the largest of the 3 bases in the game.
    • Ironically however, it isn't actually that big of a place, especially when compared to other levels.
  • Abandoned Outpost is the only base with an official name mentioned to not have "the" in its name (unless you count the name the Laboratory as its name).
  • Abandoned Outpost is the only base to have an entity within it.
  • Despite Abandoned Outpost feeling like its own level, it acts more as a second part to Level 9.
    • There has been only 2 times where the base has been officially viewed as its own level. The first is in the Part 3 release announcement, where Abandoned Outpost has an image of it as one of the levels added in the update list. The second was in Fancy's 7th Escape the Backrooms Devlog where he called it a level.
    • Because of this, in Level 9 of The Hub Mode, Abandoned Outpost is apart of the level instead of being separate.
  • If a Hub Mode-styled render of Abandoned Outpost existed in the game or elsewhere, it would likely not be the same as the Part 3 release announcement image of the base, as Level 37.2 had a different image of it added as its Hub Mode render instead of the Part 4 release announcement image of the sub-level. The difference being that the Part 4 release announcement image of the sub-level was a still image taken from a non-still scene of the sub-level from Fancy's 10th Escape the Backrooms Devlog, whilst The Hub Mode image of the sub-level came from a still image used as a teaser of the sub-level posted on September 5th, 2023 on the Escape the Backrooms Discord Server.
  • In Fancy's 7th Escape the Backrooms Devlog, he called Abandoned Outpost an abandoned M.E.G. base, which is exactly what he called the M.E.G. Base in his 4th Escape the Backrooms Devlog.
    • An interesting difference is that Abandoned Outpost is noticeably more abandoned than the M.E.G. Base which is still in decent condition.

In-Game Information

  • The base has a lot of parallels to the M.E.G. Base.
    • The M.E.G. Doors can be found in both bases in a similar concept where they are the main Door used in the base.
    • A view of what appears to be Level 0.1 from the glass window is in the base, which in the M.E.G. Base, a view of Level 0 is seen which is much safer than Level 0.1. It also matches how Level 0 is a safe level, so the M.E.G. Base is safe and still in a decent condition while on the other hand, Level 0.1 is not safe so Abandoned Outpost is not safe and in a much rougher condition. Before the base's abandonment, this parallel could be viewed with Level 9 being the not safe location.
      • Wires can be seen on the ground connecting to the base just like in the M.E.G. Base's view of Level 0.
      • The lighting is different from Level 0.1's actual lighting, which would make sense considering that Abandoned Outpost was added before Level 0.1 was. It was only one major update before Level 0.1's addition, but in general it's still 2 different updates.
      • The wall textures are not the same, because Level 0.1 has the same wall textures as Level 0.11. This Level 0.1 has Level 0's normal, undamaged wall textures.
      • If you set your graphics to low, the fog is disabled in the area.
      • There is a missing wall in the area, which can't be seen by normal means, so it probably wasn't important to add.
      • The area doesn't lead anywhere kind of similar to the actual Level 0.1 which just has Noclipping Floors on the ground that are the only way out of it, which is likely because it wasn't designed to be explored or entered since it can't be done in-game. The Noclipping Floors also don't seem to have been something planned during the time.
    • The room with the Wretch on the bed has 4 non-interactable computers in it, just like in the M.E.G. Base.
      • Because of this parallel, this could possibly be hinting at this room being the main room in the base. The M.E.G. Base was likely given 4 Computers since that's the max number of players one can have in a lobby, so if you're playing with 4 players everyone can use a Computer. It could be intentional on Abandoned Outpost's side of it because the Computers can't be used which means there's no player importance to them unlike the Computers in the M.E.G. Base.
  • The base has 2 Vents. One connects to two areas, and the other leads to nothing and is completely pointless aside from using it to hide from the Wretch.
  • The Wretch cannot chase a player into the Level 0.1 area, which can't be reached by normal means, however that's likely the reason for why this occurs.
  • There are 5 possible spawn locations for the Almond Concentrate, which means wherever they spawn, one spawn location will always not be used.
  • Once all 4 bottles of Almond Concentrate are put into the Filtering Unit, it becomes impossible to obtain anymore in the respective save file in which they were put in.
  • If you return to the base after leaving it for the first time after collecting the Administrator Card, the Wanderer will be gone, the Emergency Platform will be fully open, the Emergency Safety Shower will be put back in place (which has barely any logic aside from the Wanderer doing it), and it becomes impossible to die from things in the base since there's no longer a Wretch in the base. It is unknown what happened to the Wanderer.
    • It could also have been the case that the Wanderer is the one who did it, but this doesn't make much sense since they had nowhere else to go expect for the Level 0.1 area behind glass, which for one the glass is in the way and only a few small holes can be used to get to the other side by not breaking the glass and the glass doesn't change when revisiting the base, the actual area in the base doesn't lead anywhere, and the danger of Level 0.1 would make it a strange place to want to go. So, it's likely there wasn't an intended place for them to go.

  • A lot of biohazard signs can be found around the base.
  • It's impossible to replicate the footprints on the Emergency Platform perfectly.
  • The Emergency Safety Shower cannot be pushed back once it's pushed all the way to the Emergency Platform.
  • The projector room appears to entirely have a yellow cover covering the walls and ceiling.
  • In the projector room, there are footprints on the ground which seem to be made of a weird yellow material. This material can be seen in the same room on the ground.
    • The footprints don't match the size of the boots of the Wanderer you play as, which likely means they are just there for show.
    • The footprints seem to be a way of hinting that there was once Wanderers here.
  • A yellow warning sign can be seen in the projector room warning people about the rest of the base itself. It says, "WARNING HEAVY CHEMICAL USAGE AREA AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY NO ENTRY".
    • This isn't an actual problem in the base itself because despite the heavy usage of chemicals in the base, it can't harm you. Logically, this could be because of the hazmat suit players wear.
  • A dead body stored in a yellow cover with a zipper can be found in the projector room.
    • This isn't the first time a dead body can be found in the game, as there are also dead bodies in zone 3 of Level 3.
  • The projector always has a dim light coming through it, even after the projector video is finished.
  • There is no logic behind how the projector automatically plays a video once someone enters the projector room.
  • The projector video at one point shows an image of the base, specifically the room with the Wretch on the bed. Weirdly enough, in the image in the video, the Wretch is not there, the blood below the bed is gone, and the water cooler knocked down nearby the platform is not knocked down.
    • This seems to show the base before it became abandoned.
  • The projector video shows the former Wanderer model, since Abandoned Outpost was added to the game when the former model was still being used.

The model shown in that video was likely not made when the projector video was made since the first time it was shown was a blurred teaser of it in Fancy's 7th Escape the Backrooms Devlog, which was uploaded on June 30th, 2023, a bit of time after the release of Part 3 on June 15th, 2023 which added the base.

    • "Emergency Platform" is said, but the subtitles say "EMERGENCY PLATFORMS". Only one Emergency Platform can be found in the base, however.
    • "close" is said but the subtitles say "CLOSED".
    • "4 full bottles of Concentrate" is said but the subtitles say "4 BOTTLE OF CONCENTRATE".
    • The fourth one is when the sentence "Manually opening the Platform can cause harm to the affected person or others in the laboratory." is said but the subtitles cut it off 3 words early and says, "MANUALLY OPENING THE PLATFORM CAN CAUSE HARM TO THE AFFECTED PERSON OR OTHERS." This could've been due to the space on the screen.
  • Some of the Wretch's footstep sounds don't make much sense with the ground of the base since they are wooden sounds and the ground is entirely concrete.
  • The rough floor seen in majority of the base is a floor design that can also be seen on the Character Select screen and in the Bunker.
  • The state of the ceiling (not in the first hallway or the projector room) in the base looks really similar to the state of the M.E.G. base area in the Bunker and Overgrowth's ceilings (excluding the base area in Overgrowth).
  • 3 exit signs can be found around the base. 2 are red and appear above 2 specific Doors on the hallway side of them, and 1 that's green which is right above the hallway leading to the rest of the base, which might be indicating the hallway leading to the exit.
  • If you have the Wanderer you're playing as stand in or look at while inside any water leaking from the pipes in the base with the game's graphics set to ultra or epic, the game will get a bit laggy.
    • If this is done with high or lower graphics, it will not occur.
  • On the platform that has the bed with the Wretch in it, you can see the numbers 0-1, which could be referring to Level 0.1, which would make sense since there's a view of it in the base.
    • If you shine a Flashlight on the numbers, they will appear yellow.
    • It doesn't make much sense why the numbers would be right next to something not nearby or associated with the view of Level 0.1, so it could likely be referring to something else.
  • The Wretch on the bed has white eyes instead of black ones which every other Wretch has.
  • There is an invisible wall on the platform that has the bed with the Wretch in it, above the bars surrounding the platform, and on the table where an Almond Concentrate can spawn, which seems to be there to keep players from getting past the area where the bed is. It's possible to get to the other side by crouch jumping onto the bed, or in between the space between the bed and the chair.
    • Crouch jumping is a technique where you press CTRL to crouch in the middle of a jump, which allows a player to get on top of a bunch of things they aren't intended to be on.
  • In the farthest chemical storage room from the start of the base, you can shine a Flashlight on the left and right walls to reveal a biohazard sign. Before shining the Flashlight, the biohazard sign can be seen but not very clearly.
  • The Inventory has 3 slots for Items to carry by using the 1 2 and 3 keys but there's 4 bottles of Almond Concentrate to put into the Filtering Unit. This means if you have all 4 bottles of Concentrate, you'll need to put in 1 before you can have all 3 ready in the carrying Inventory slots.
  • The moment the Wretch turns back into a Human is hidden by the gas from the Emergency Safety Shower. Without the gas, you can see that the Wretch model gets instantly replaced by the Wanderer model.
    • This is likely the case because modeling out the Wretch transformation into a Human wasn't necessary since it was just hidden by the gas.
  • When the Wretch is turned back into a Human, only 2 sides of the Emergency Platform open up. This seems to be the case because of the Emergency Safety Shower being in the way of one side, to make sure the Wanderer has something to lie on, and there needs to be a way for players to collect the Administrator Card.
  • The Wretch's Crowbar is completely gone when it's turned back into a Human. There is no explanation on how or why it's gone.
  • If you leave the base after turning the Wretch back into a Human and if the Administrator Card isn't collected, they will turn back into a Wretch once you re-enter the base. This doesn't stop until the Administrator Card is collected.
  • Once the Wretch is turned back into a Human, the Administrator Card can't be collected until the smoke from the Emergency Safety Shower is cleared.

Lore & Theories

  • This seems to be the most Lore significant area in the entire game, considering the heavy importance of Wretches here, an outbreak is mentioned in Level 9's Hub entry to have occurred here, and the projector video mentions some things that don't occur in the base mission and can't occur when in the base.
    • The things it mentions that don't occur in the mission include an accidental contamination event, the Emergency Safety Shower being moved to a different area, manually opening the Emergency Platform, and cleaning the equipment and replacing all the materials before leaving.
  • There might be a reason for Abandoned Outpost being located in Level 9.
    • One possibility is that the Wretches in Level 9 might have already been in the level, which would probably make M.E.G. curious on them and want to make a base in the level to study them.
    • Another possibility is that the Wretches in Level 9 were members of the base who became Wretches from the outbreak mentioned in Level 9's Hub entry. The only evidence to support the Wretches in Level 9 being members of the base is the Crowbars they have. Crowbars cannot be found in Level 9, but 3 Crowbars can be found in Abandoned Outpost. They're likely just there so players have something to break open the 2 Vents found in the base, but for all we know it could have Lore significance.
  • The base is very hidden, which seems quite important considering the house it exists in is surrounded by a metal fence, the gate can only be opened by finding 3 Computers and enabling them which are all scattered in the houses of Level 9, and the idea of putting the base in the largest house in Level 9.
  • There might be Lore importance as for why Sanity doesn't drain in the base. The place seemed to be designed as a safe place just used for studying and if it would remain safe, Sanity would have to be kept from draining. It could also just be a gameplay thing to keep the base from being too hard to complete since the base has Wretch safety features such as the Emergency Platform and Emergency Safety Shower so Sanity might have been something in mind for the past inhabitants of the base, especially since this is a place where they studied Wretches.
  • The Hub entry for Level 9 mentions there being reports of an outbreak in the base. It also mentioned how this cannot be confirmed.
    • In the base, it very much seems like something happened that caused its abandoned appearance, and it could very much have been the outbreak.
  • Fancy implied that running out of Sanity is the cause for becoming a Wretch in his 5th Escape the Backrooms Devlog, which at first it would seem like the Wretch in the base became a Wretch inside the base itself, however that cannot be the case because it's impossible for Sanity to drain in the base. This likely means the Wanderer who turned into a Wretch became a Wretch in a different place, most likely Level 9 since it has the highest Sanity drain rate and Abandoned Outpost is located next to Level 9.

Audio

  • The ambience of the base is stored in a folder in the game files with other similar sounding ambiences that appear in other places in the game. All the ambiences seem to have been created by the same people, because they all sound somewhat similar to each other.
    • The other ambiences that can be found in here include the ambience of the Bunker, Overgrowth, The End lights out event/Level 55.1, Level 10's 2nd ambience/Level 52, floor 2 and 3 of Level 188, and Level 37.2's 2 2nd ambiences.
    • All the ambiences in here follow a style in the file name, which goes "PLSNC_ND_LOW_(nickname)_LOOP_(1 or 2)". The nickname in place for Abandoned Outpost's ambience is "Shades_Unblest", and it uses the number 1 as its loop number.
  • Not only is the audio file of the projector video titled "LabVideo", but the video file of the projector video is titled "LabVideo".
